Helping you make environmentally responsible purchases that make sense.

84% of students aged 18-34 feel responsible for solving important environmental issues. At the same time, these students generate more avoidable waste than any other demographic.

This is largely because when the average post-secondary student goes to the grocery store every week, most of the products they buy are unsustainable. Why? They simply do not know that reasonable alternatives exist. Moreover, these individuals are not motivated to choose these alternatives solely based on environmental impact. They need to be incentivized by factors like cost, convenience and personal preferences.

This got us thinking… How might we encourage Canadian students in post-secondary institutions to purchase more environmentally responsible consumables, meaning products they usually buy from grocery stores?

The answer to this question: our mobile app, ThinkTwice.


Take a Picture

First off, ThinkTwice allows the user to go into a grocery store and simply take a picture of the product that they want to buy.


Product Details & Alternatives

Right away, the user gets immediate information on the sustainability of the packaging and the end of life of the product they scanned, meaning whether it's compostable, recyclable, or belongs in the landfill.

Next, our app provides three more environmentally responsible alternatives to the original product, based on the factors of cost, convenience and personal preferences.

The first alternative is an option that has a similar or even lower cost than the original product. The second is based on convenience, meaning an alternative that is closest to where the user currently is. The third option is one that is likely to be most appealing to that specific user, based on their past purchases. For each of these options, we provide a rating of how environmentally responsible the alternative is.


Community

Based on the rating of the alternative the user chooses, they get a certain number of points.

We use this points system in the Community page of our app, in which users can compete with friends they’ve added and other university teams.


Home

On this page, we motivate our users to continue to Think Twice by awarding them with badges and showing them helpful statistics about their progress, like: you saved $100, or you protected 5 trees.


Explore

Explore displays up-and-coming environmentally-friendly companies.

Just like that, users can learn about the sustainability of these companies’ products, which may alter their future purchasing decisions.


On July 19th, 2019, the six of us pitched ThinkTwice to a group of angel investors, as a part of the SHAD program at York University.

We worked extremely hard to create an exceptional product during the three weeks leading up to this day. We started by conducting our own research about the issue of waste management and consumerism, specifically pertaining to university students.

During SHAD, we not only delivered a prototype of our app, but also had the opportunity to hear valuable feedback from professors, investors, and other mentors about our company.

This project was led and managed by Suhana Nadeem, a student from Mississauga, ON. She’s taken this initiative beyond SHAD, and is in the process of further developing ThinkTwice.
